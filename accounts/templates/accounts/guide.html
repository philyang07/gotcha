Assassin is a popular game often played with large groups of people with the simple premise:

  • All players are assigned a target (one-to-one) that they need to 'eliminate'
  • Once a player eliminates their target, they get a new target: the target of their original target
  • The winner is the last person standing (or whoever has the most eliminations by the end of a given time frame etc.)

The method of elimination is up to the decision of the game organiser and its players, but it must be completely harmless.

An 'open list' contains players that can be eliminated by any other player, and is useful for speeding up the progress of the game. A common rule is that if a player is inactive for 24 hours, they will be put onto the open list.

How to use the manager?

Firstly, the organiser of the game creates a game. There are 3 stages to running a game.

  1. The registration stage: new players register for the game via an access code set by the game organiser
  2. Target assignment: the players will be assigned their targets and the players can now check who their target is
  3. The elimination stage: players can now eliminate their target and submit their completed assignment
    • Submission requires acquiring the target's secret code and submitting it on the website for validation

What tools are provided by the manager?

  1. Open list management
    • Set a custom duration for how long players can be inactive before being put onto the open list
    • Manually putting players onto the open list regardless of their inactivity
  2. Manual eliminations of players, including revival if necessary
  3. Scrambling of targets, if you want to spice things up
  4. A visual representation of the player-target chain
